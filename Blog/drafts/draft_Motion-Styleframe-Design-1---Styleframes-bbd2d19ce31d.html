0. The Definition of Styleframes

Styleframe is a static visual that encompasses the entire art direction of a movie or animation. So the viewer of the styleframe should be able to understand the general aesthetic of your movie and the final look and feel. Here are some useful websites,

  • http://artofstyleframe.com/#/home
  1. Elements of Design

Elements of design are pillars of visual communication and object creation. Without them, we are not able to develop a visual language in order to communicate or send a message. The design elements include

  • Lines: start from a point, draw for a distance, and never come back.
  • Shapes: start from a point, draw for a distance, but come back to the starting point
  • Forms: the lights and shadows on the shapes to show the volume and the depth. From is the 3-dimensional representation of shapes including shadow and light in order to add the volume information. It can be geometric and natural while it is measured by height, width, and depth.
  • Texture: the details on the surfaces of forms or shapes
  • color: the lights reflect from the surface of a shape

3. Types of Textures

  • Natural texture: created naturally by the environment 
  • Artificial texture: created by humans or machines with human controls
  • Organic texture: these are similar to the natural textures, but they are irregular and asymmetrical
  • Geometric texture: these are textures that seem more like shapes
  • Tactical texture: these are textures that can be described by the sense of touch
  • Visual texture: looks more like designs and they have no sense of a real surface

4. Function of Shapes

Different shapes serve different functions. The basic shapes include, 

  • Cube: means stable and rigid. Therefore, most designers use cubes for rough and rigorous layouts. What’s more, some 3D artists also use cubes and rectangles in order to build some 3D installation arts.
  • Circle: protection and peace. The cycle is more dynamic than the cube because it contains the feelings of the movements. Cycles can help you contain different or similar elements to make a compacted or united result. Moreover, the cycles can create highlights of some elements.
  • Triangle: strength and power, and it can make conflicts. It can also show the direction in the layout. The triangle can show the important elements that we would like to make the readers focus on in the design. 

5. Color Palette

When we mixing more colors, we are going to build emotional relationships. We are able to find lots of resources about creating a color palette. For example, 

  • Adobe Color: upload a picture to see its color palette
  • Coolors.co: generate our own palette by locking and randomly selecting
  • Colorshemedesigner: a flexible palette generate and it is even nice because we can create color palettes for color blind people

If we have two opposite colors in the color wheel, it seems that they will be more like enemies. However, if we use some analog hues, they will look more friendly. 

6. Emotions of Shapes

Different shapes can also deliver different feelings. 

For example, in the first picture, we have an organic shape and a small circle. It seems like that the big shape with a similar color wants to hug the small red circle like the mother and child.

In the second picture, we have a strong contrast of the size and there is hardly any balance. It seems like the father dominates the son. 

In the last picture, we have a triangle that is stronger than a circle. However, they have opposite colors and the color of the triangle seems more peaceful. If we put them together, it seems that they will fight with each other. 

Now, let’s see some references. 

In the first reference, the rectangle is used as the base of the circle. The triangle gives us the direction so that our eyes will focus on the circle. Because they have the same texture, there’s no contrast among them. The rectangular has a different texture but it doesn’t bother because it is merely used as the basis. 

In the second reference, you can see these chaotic things in the background but they are not a mess. We can find out that the background consists of different rectangles and triangles. In that composition, there are shapes inside shapes, and because they are in similar colors, it creates the rhythm and we can feel that they are united. In the center, we have a sphere that has volume and depth. And it stands out from the background because of its wooden texture. 

In the third reference, the design makes our eyes focusing on a specific object. In terms of the colors, the red one is the strongest color and it dominates the green one and the magenta. In terms of the shapes, the combination of circle and cube creates a nice dynamic new shape that triggers the domination. What’s more, behind the red object, there is a bright area that makes our eyes focusing on it. 

In the last reference, we have a 100% black and white image. We have the shape of a triangle in the middle which points to the direction of the movement. In this way, the circle looks more peaceful. Two smaller circles also have the same meaning. Right now, the focus point should be the sphere in the center.
