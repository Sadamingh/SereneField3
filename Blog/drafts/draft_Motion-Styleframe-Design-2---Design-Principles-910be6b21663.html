  1. The Definition of Design Principles

Design principles are the applicable fundamentals on how to use the design elements in order to create a visual experience. These include,

  • Rhythm
  • Unity/Harmony
  • Balance
  • Emphasis
  • Contrast

(1) Rhythm

Rhythm happens when you repeat elements to create consistency and visual interest. For example, 

Commonly, we have two types of rhythm, the regular rhythm, and the random rhythm. The regular rhythm is a repetition of elements of design plus typography. And the random rhythm happens also when we repeat the elements of design, but we have to give some variations like modifying the size, the color, the front, the prospective, and etc. 

Based on the regular rhythm, we can move forward to the definition of patterns. Patterns are parts of the design principles, and they are basically a design where the same or similar shapes are being repeated at regular intervals over a surface. The patterns can be 2D and 3D, and they can even be some aerial geographics.

Now, let’s continue to talk about random rhythm. We have said that the random rhythm happens when we repeat design in a nonlinear and specific way, while we alter some of its properties. Let’s now continue to discuss movement and flow. A lot of people are considering movement and flow as separate design principle. However, we do consider that it is one part of the rhythm. Photographers, 3D artists, and painters use lines and curves to create movements and flows. Moreover, they can also be used to direct eyes and trace the pictures. 

In fact, the flows can be used to show the ground and direct your eyes to the proper position. For example, the following pictures on the left side are all creating nice lines for heading towards the mountains or the sun. In the picture on the right side, we can find out that a spiral has been created towards the center of the picture and it nicely directs our eyes into the depth of the picture.

(2) Unity and Harmony

Unity and harmony happen when you coherence all the parts in the picture in order to make them work together. If we treat these two concepts separately, unity is that you can find the same elements of design throughout the work of art, while harmony is defined as similar elements of design throughout the work of art. But these two concepts have one same goal, which is coherence. 

Basically, unity and harmony are made by proximity, grouping, overlapping, continuity, and containment. Proximity happens when you place similar elements close to each other. Common icons like Windows, Disney, BMW, and the switch have consisted of some similar elements close to each other.

In the last poster, we have that straight light goes through the sky, and with the number 5 next to it, we can see the meaning is the number 15.
