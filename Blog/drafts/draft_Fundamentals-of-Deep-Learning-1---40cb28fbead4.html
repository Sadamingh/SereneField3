  1. Basic Concepts of the Machine Learning

When we say machine learning, that’s almost what they mean when they say supervised machine learning. The goal of machine learning is to come up with some kind of model that given a particular record or anything we want to make a prediction or a classification.

If we take in a record or an entity, and the model is going to yield a numerical result, then it is called a prediction. The opposite is that instead of using a numerical result, we are going to yield a class or a category result. Then this is called classification.

To think about the regression and the classification, you maybe consider them to be totally different things at the first glance. But actually, it turns out that they are just two sides of the same coin when it comes to implementation. The real difference between these two concepts is that the regressor draws a line through the data, while the classifier draws lines that are decision boundaries.

Training a model means capturing the relationship between feature vectors and a target variable, given a training data set. The feature vector x means a set of features or attributes characterizing an entity, such as square footage or the number of bedrooms or bathrooms. The target y is either a scalar value like rent price (regressor) or an integer indicating “creditworthy” or “it is not cancer” (classifier).
