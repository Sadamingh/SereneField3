2. Baby F Major Chord

The F major chord is the most difficult chord to play because we have to use a full bar on the first fret, which is a little more difficult because we have to really push those strings down to get a good sound. However, until our fingers are stronger, we may need to hold on of playing that chord.

But we can still play the F major in an easier way, and the fingers are as follows. Here, we are only doing a partial bar with,

  • the first finger pressing the first two strings which is C and F
  • the second finger pressing the second fret on the third string which is the A note
  • the third finger plays the fourth string on the third fret which is the F note

Put it back on, the notes of the partial F major chord are F-A-C-F. And note that the first finger has to play both of the C and F notes. You may find this difficult and some things we can try to move the finger to the side to get a little bit more leverage with the thumb underneath.
